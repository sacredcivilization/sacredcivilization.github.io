Volume IV shatters the foundational myth of Western civilizational historiography: the "Golden Age collapse" — the claim that Islamic intellectual civilization rose, peaked, and fell, with its knowledge preserved only insofar as Europe absorbed it through translation.

Against this myth, Volume IV traces the continuous hidden architecture of transmission from the pre-Diluvian science of Idris through every apparent "collapse" to the living custodial networks of the present. Each apparent collapse is revealed as a capture event — a moment when the state seized the visible infrastructure while the genuine transmission went underground into the chain.

Five Chapters + Final Synthesis

  • Chapter I. The First Inheritance: The Science of Idris — the triple science (cosmological, social, material) and its pre-Diluvian origination; the chain's first link
  • Chapter II. Karbala and the Cryptographic Custody — the Sahifa Sajjadiyya as encrypted transmission map; how software survives hardware failure; the batini caliphate underground
  • Chapter III. The Bibliographic Trap: The Bayt al-Hikma — the Abbasid state apparatus as designed epistemic capture; Hunayn ibn Ishaq as double agent; what was translated and what was amputated
  • Chapter IV. The Toledo Theft — the Latin extraction syndicate; the Latinization filter; the selective theft of apparatus without metaphysical framework; Aquinas and the debt never acknowledged
  • Chapter V + Final Synthesis. The Scriptorium Vault Today — the living custodial networks; the SCRA as digital prototype of non-institutional transmission; the chain that empire could never extinguish
