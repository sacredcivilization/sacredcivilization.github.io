Working Papers

Seven DOI-registered academic papers — open-access, Zenodo-archived

The SCRA Working Papers series constitutes the primary academic output of the Sacred Civilization Research Archive. Each paper is peer-quality in scope and methodology, permanently archived on Zenodo with assigned DOIs, and freely accessible at alvidscriptorium.com/research/. All papers are authored by Saad Khizar Bosal, Framework Architect, Sacred Civilization Research Archive (SCRA), Mandi Bahauddin, Punjab, Pakistan.

Working Paper 01  ·  Islamic Intellectual History

The Sassanid-Syriac-Toledo Knowledge Transmission Chain

A forensic reconstruction of the seven-century knowledge corridor through which Greek, Persian, and Indian learning entered the Latin West via Gondishapur, Bayt al-Hikma, and Toledo. Four mechanisms of erasure. Implications for the Huntington thesis. ~4,800 words  ·  14 citations.

Working Paper 02  ·  Early Islamic Political History

Saqifa Banu Sa'ida and the Structural Isolation of the Prophetic House

Cross-confessional analysis using Sahih al-Bukhari alongside Shia primary sources. Ghadir Khumm (Tirmidhi, Musnad Ahmad). The Raziyyat al-Khamis (Bukhari No. 114). Five mechanisms of structural isolation. Tragic hero framework. ~4,400 words  ·  12 citations.

DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20457201 Zenodo ↗ Academia.edu ↗

Working Paper 03  ·  Civilizational Theory

Against the Clash: Deconstructing Huntington Through the Islamo-Christian Framework

Four-level demolition of Huntington: empirical (Henderson & Tucker, 2001), methodological (Sen, 2006), ontological (Mulla Sadra's Tashkik al-Wujud), and political (Said, 2001). Bulliet's Islamo-Christian Civilization counter-framework. ~3,800 words  ·  10 citations.

DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20457848 Zenodo ↗ Academia.edu ↗

Working Paper 04  ·  Islamic Intellectual History  ·  Alid Transmission

The School of Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq and the Abbasid Extraction

The Islamic Golden Age was built on knowledge transmitted by the Sixth Imam's school — Jabir ibn Hayyan, Abu Hanifa, Malik ibn Anas — while the Abbasid state simultaneously eliminated the Imams. Forensic analysis using Corbin's zahir-batin framework and the Ba'alist Capture Mechanism typology. ~5,200 words  ·  15 citations.

DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20466707 Zenodo ↗ Academia.edu ↗

Working Paper 05  ·  Quranic Ontology  ·  Islamic Philosophical Theology

Haq and Batil: The Quranic Ontology of the Ba'alist Capture Mechanism

Seven structural attributes of Batil derived from five defining ayat through Shia tafseer — Al-Mizan (Tabatabai), Tafsir al-Qummi, Al-Burhan (al-Bahrani). Imam Ali's admixture doctrine from Nahj al-Balagha as the ontological explanation of why Batil persists by coating itself with Haq. ~5,500 words  ·  17 citations.

DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20466709 Zenodo ↗ Academia.edu ↗

Working Paper 06  ·  Political Theology  ·  Islamic Intellectual History  ·  South Asian Studies

The Indus Thesis: Iqbal's Persian Synthesis and the Ba'alist Capture of Pakistan's Foundational Ideology

Pakistan's authentic foundational ideology was Iqbalian — rooted in the Khorasan-Persian synthesis, expressed through the Chishti-Sufi tradition of the Indus basin, and geographically specified in the 1930 Allahabad Address. The post-1977 Deobandi-Wahhabi transformation analyzed as a Type III Ba'alist Capture: Cold War-funded, Arabia-rooted, and geographically external. Seven Attributes of Batil applied. ~7,000 words  ·  17 citations.

DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20467615 Zenodo ↗ Academia.edu ↗ Full Text ↗

Working Paper 07  ·  Islamic Jurisprudence  ·  Political Theology  ·  Intellectual History of Sunnism

The Sealed Room: Ibn Taymiyyah's Jurisprudential Architecture of Ba'alist Capture

The Abbasid-Mamluk construction of a juridical immune system: the Ijtihad Shield immunizes the state's foundational violence from accountability; the Bid'ah Sword prosecutes the authentic tradition's memory of that violence. Ibn Taymiyyah's Minhaj as-Sunnah as the formal doctrinal manual of Ba'alist Capture — creating the legal loophole in which the state's mistakes earn spiritual reward while the people's grief earns condemnation. The criminalization of Karbala mourning as political suppression of testimony. ~7,500 words  ·  17 citations.

DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20467617 Zenodo ↗ Academia.edu ↗ Full Text ↗
