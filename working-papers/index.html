The Mughal Synthesis and Its Collapse: Akbarʼs Din-e-Ilahi, Dara Shikohʼs Majmaʿ al-Bahḥrayn, and Aurangzebʼs Baʼalist Capture of the Indus Batin (933–1118 AH / 1526–1707 CE)

The Mughal period (933–1118 AH / 1526–1707 CE) as the supreme civilizational event of the Indus basin. The Timurid-Safavid-Sufi inheritance (Babur, Humayun, Chishti silsila). Akbar's four-stage synthesis: Ibadat Khana (1575–1582 CE), Din-e-Ilahi, the Mahzar of 1579, Abul Fazl's cosmological framework. Dara Shikoh's two-text programme: Majmaʿ al-Bahḥrayn (1655 CE) — the Quranic-Vedantic convergence map — and Sirr-e-Akbar (1657 CE). Aurangzeb's five reversals as Baʼalist Capture: closure of the Ibadat Khana, reversal of Din-e-Ilahi, execution of Dara Shikoh (the Indus Saqifa, 1659 CE), Fatawa-e-Alamgiri imposition, destruction of Chishti devotional architecture. The genealogy chain: Ahmad Sirhindi → Aurangzeb → Shah Wali Allah → Deoband 1867 → Zia → JUI-F → Munir's resistance. Intizār Archive Verdict: the most advanced zahir-batin synthesis in post-Saqifa Sunni history — terminated by Baʼalist Capture from within. ~10,000 words  ·  32 citations.
